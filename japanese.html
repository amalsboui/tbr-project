Japanese Books

Discover the enchanting realm of Japanese literature. Immerse yourself in a diverse array of genres, from mystery to romance, and delve into the rich cultural tapestry of Japan. With captivating storytelling perfect for all readers, our curated selection invites you to explore the intricacies of Japanese narratives and themes.

  • Norwegian wood by Haruki Murakami

    " And once the storm is over, you won't remember how you made it through, how you managed to survive. You won't even be sure, whether the storm is really over. But one thing is certain. When you come out of the storm, you won't be the same person who walked in. That's what this storm's all about."

  • Before the coffee gets cold by Toshikazu Kawaguchi

    "Time might separate people, but it doesn’t make them forget."

  • Kitchen by Banana Yashimoto

    "The more I love humanity in general, the less I love man in particular."

  • All the lovers in the night by Mieko Kawakami

    "Even when we think we’ve reached the depths of despair, we can always find something to be thankful for, no matter how small."

  • Idol, Burning by Rin Usami

    "Sometimes, the brightest flames are fueled by the darkest shadows."

  • The Hole by Hiroko Oyamada

    “As I started to walk, it seemed like nothing around me was moving. The trees were as still as in a photograph, and the windows of all the houses were shut tight. There were no people around. No cats, no dogs, no crows. There wasn’t a single sparrow in the sky. My eyes were tingling from the heat. Once the water from Grandpa’s hose was too far away to hear, the only sound left was the cicadas...”

  • Lonely Castle in the Mirror by Mizuki Tsujimara

    "Those who feel the deepest loneliness also have the ability to find the deepest connections."

  • Mild Vertigo by Mieko Kanai

    "why do people need to do all of this to commemorate their wedding and reassure themselves that they’re married, that’s what I want to know."

  • Revenge by Yoko Ogawa

    "I had no intention of running after him. It was as though he had already gone somewhere far away, and I could run and run but I would never catch him."

  • Days at the Morisalri Bookshop by Satoshi Yagisawa

    "in those days, I really was like a butterfly waiting patiently to come out of its chrysalis. As I turned page after page, I was waiting for my chance to take flight."

  • Life Ceremony by Sayaka Murata

    "I mean, normal is a type of madness, isn't it? I think it's just that the only madness society allows is called normal."
